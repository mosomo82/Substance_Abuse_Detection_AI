## 6. Technical Reflection

### What worked well
The **multi-layer technical narrative** in the final report was the project's strongest communication asset. By meticulously documenting how Layer 1 (Rules) filtered for Layer 3 (LLM), and how Layer 4 (BERT) learned from the Ensemble, we were able to present a complex system as a logical, optimized pipeline. The "Ethics & Privacy" section was also critical; several domain reviewers noted that the Irreversible PII Masking was the project's most important "real-world" feature.

The **HITL Audit (6/10 approval)** was a grounding influence on the project. While the machine metrics were promising, manual review revealed that the ensemble still struggles with sarcasm and colloquial metaphors (e.g., "fired up"). Identifying these 4 failure modes allowed us to create a more honest "Future Improvements" section, which increased the overall credibility of the submission.

### Challenges and lessons learned
The **LaTeX transition** for the final report was more time-consuming than anticipated. Converting the complex tables and multi-layer architecture diagrams into a one-page-friendly LaTeX template required significant troubleshooting. Future teams should finalize the markdown structure at least 48 hours before the deadline to ensure the PDF compilation is stable.

Capturing the **diversity of street slang** in the non-punitive summaries remains difficult. The LLM occasionally uses medicalized language that doesn't match the community-led discourse in the Erowid-grounded summaries. Fine-tuning the RAG system to use more community-aligned harm-reduction terminology would improve the dashboard's resonance with actual health analysts.
