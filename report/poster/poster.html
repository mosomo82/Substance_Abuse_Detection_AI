Problem
A Crisis Without Real-Time Surveillance

The U.S. substance abuse crisis causes 80,000+ deaths annually, yet traditional surveillance — hospital records, population surveys — carries lag times of months to years.

Online forums hold real-time, high-volume discourse on substance use, distress, and addiction. But this text is noisy, slang-heavy, and entangled with personally identifiable information.

We bridge this gap with a privacy-preserving, population-level decision-support system: ingest social text → detect risk signals → correlate with CDC data → surface analyst-ready summaries.

Preprocessing
Four-Stage Privacy Pipeline

Raw text passes through four stages before any model sees it:

  1. HTML & Noise Removal — BeautifulSoup + regex strip markup, URLs, special characters
  2. PII Scrubbing — spaCy NER masks names, locations, phones, e-mails; no identity stored
  3. Slang Normalization — Curated lexicon maps colloquialisms to standard substance tokens
  4. Deduplication — Semantic hashing removes near-duplicates (Jaccard ≥ 0.85)
Ethics
Privacy-Preserving by Design
Anonymization Irreversible PII masking at ingestion — no identity stored or transmitted
Population-level only Dashboard architecturally blocked from surfacing individual records
Non-punitive design Scoped to public health planning; structurally incompatible with law enforcement
Human-in-the-Loop All signals labeled for analyst review — final decisions remain with humans
Bias-aware Lexicon and seed-bank biases documented; HITL audit flags systematic errors
Transparent All code, datasets, and model decisions open-source and auditable

K-Means (k=8) clustering on UMAP-reduced embeddings identifies evolving behavioral sub-populations. Temporal spike detection uses rolling z-scores (threshold Z > 2.5) binned monthly.

Example System Output
RAG Analyst Summary

A 300% surge in high-risk opioid signals was detected across Cluster 0 (4,617 posts) between October 12–19. Discourse features fentanyl analogs mislabeled as prescription stimulants. Recommended action: Alert harm-reduction networks to distribute updated test strips and issue clinician advisories.

All summaries are aggregate and population-level. Individual posts are never surfaced. Final intervention decisions remain with healthcare professionals.

Future Work
Roadmap
  • Live stream integration — Reddit/Twitter APIs for near-real-time monitoring rather than batch processing
  • Dynamic lexicon updates — Auto-surface emerging slang (e.g. xylazine-class terms) from high-risk clusters
  • Geospatial heatmaps — County/state-level risk maps from anonymized location tokens
  • Active HITL feedback loop — Analyst approve/reject votes feed back into BERT fine-tuning
  • Multimodal expansion — Image signals from pill photography and harm-reduction meme culture
